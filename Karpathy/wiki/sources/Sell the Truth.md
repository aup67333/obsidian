# Sell the Truth

## What It Is

`Sell the Truth` is a Naval Podcast transcript where [[wiki/entities/Naval Ravikant|Naval Ravikant]] and [[wiki/entities/Nivi|Nivi]] discuss selling, persuasion, leadership, recruiting, dealmaking, and motivation. Its central idea is that high-quality selling is less about tactics and more about truthfully conveying something you deeply understand and genuinely believe.

Raw source: [raw/incoming/Sell the Truth.md](../../raw/incoming/Sell%20the%20Truth.md)

## Why It Matters

This source is useful for a personal wiki because it reframes persuasion as an integrity problem: the durable form of sales is credibility, not pressure. It also links sales to broader ideas about leadership, motivation, high-trust teams, optionality, and nonlinear upside.

## Key Takeaways

- Credibility matters more than sales technique; sophisticated people can see through tactics.
- The best sales pitch is often just accurate, enthusiastic explanation of something true.
- [[wiki/concepts/Rational Empathy|Rational Empathy]] means reasoning your way into the other person's position before reinforcing your own.
- [[wiki/concepts/Charisma|Charisma]] is described as projecting confidence and love, or power plus good intentions.
- Leadership differs from management: management tells people what to do; leadership makes them want to do it.
- Small, high-trust teams are framed as modern [[wiki/concepts/Stag Hunt|Stag Hunt]] arrangements: people constrain options together to pursue larger shared upside.
- Bad deals are dangerous because contracts constrain future options.
- In [[wiki/concepts/Nonlinear Returns|Nonlinear Returns]], the future upside can dwarf present disputes, so focus on preserving time, reputation, peace, and optionality.
- [[wiki/concepts/Good Obsessions|Good Obsessions]] are intellectual or creative fixations that can become lasting parts of a person's capability.

## Important Claims

- [[wiki/claims/Credibility Beats Sales Tactics|Credibility Beats Sales Tactics]] - If someone feels sold to, they resist; trust and honesty are more durable than persuasion tricks.
- [[wiki/claims/Bad Deals Constrain Future Optionality|Bad Deals Constrain Future Optionality]] - A bad investor, contract, or partner can trap a founder long after the immediate pressure has passed.
- [[wiki/claims/Nonlinear Returns Favor Upside Over Small Spoils|Nonlinear Returns Favor Upside Over Small Spoils]] - In power-law environments, fighting over small present gains is often less important than preserving future upside.
