# High Trust Teams

## Definition

High Trust Teams are small groups of competent people who can rely on each other enough to pursue difficult shared missions.

## Why It Matters

In [[wiki/sources/Sell the Truth|Sell the Truth]], Naval describes humans as craving small-group missions where everyone pulls their weight and gets to operate near maximum creativity. This is central to his recruiting pitch for startups.

## Key Points

- Team size should be the minimum needed for the scope of the mission.
- The bar for teammates should remain very high.
- Trust, reputation, and shared mission let people attempt work that would be impossible alone.
- Recruiting can sell the quality of the people as much as the product or market.
