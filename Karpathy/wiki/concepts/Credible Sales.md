# Credible Sales

## Definition

Credible Sales is persuasion grounded in truth, competence, and trust rather than pressure, manipulation, or rote technique.

## Why It Matters

In [[wiki/sources/Sell the Truth|Sell the Truth]], Naval argues that people resist feeling sold to, especially highly capable people who can see through tactics. The durable alternative is to become credible: understand the thing, believe it, explain it clearly, and stay detached enough to move on when it does not resonate.

## Key Points

- The seller should understand what the other person wants before trying to persuade.
- Authenticity and knowledge matter more than memorized frameworks.
- A good salesperson may steer someone away from a bad deal, increasing trust for the right deal.
- Not every person should be convinced; a mismatch is a reason to move on.
- The strongest version of sales is conveying what is already true and exciting to the seller.
