# Selfish Honesty

## Definition

Selfish Honesty is the practice of being objective and truthful because it improves one's own decisions.

## Why It Matters

In [[wiki/sources/Sell the Truth|Sell the Truth]], Naval says he wants to be honest because he is already wrong often; without objectivity, he would be even more wrong. Honesty is therefore not only moral but instrumentally useful.

## Key Points

- Objectivity requires getting the ego out of the way.
- Advice can be more useful when it helps the other person hear themselves more clearly.
- Honesty is a practical defense against self-deception.
- Selfish honesty supports credible persuasion because it reduces hidden agenda.
