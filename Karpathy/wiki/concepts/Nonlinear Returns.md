# Nonlinear Returns

## Definition

Nonlinear Returns are outcomes where the upside can be many times larger than typical results, making the best opportunities disproportionately important.

## Why It Matters

Naval argues that in technology and investing, future upside can be 100x, 1,000x, or more. This changes how one should think about deals: preserving time, reputation, and optionality for large upside may matter more than fighting over small present spoils.

## Key Points

- Power-law environments reward the biggest winners disproportionately.
- Time and optionality should be spent only where large upside is plausible.
- Small disputes may not be worth the mental health cost unless principle or future deterrence requires fighting.
- The best outcome is not purely financial; peace, happiness, and openness to the next thing also matter.
