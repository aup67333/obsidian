# Rational Empathy

## Definition

Rational Empathy is reasoning your way into another person's position, identifying why it might be valid, and then responding from that understanding.

## Why It Matters

Naval uses this idea to explain his version of "yes, and." The "yes" is not a trick; it comes from finding the valid part of the other person's view before adding or defending one's own position.

## Key Points

- It starts from the assumption that smart people usually have reasons for their views.
- It does not require pretending to agree when one does not.
- It helps disagreement become more precise and less ego-driven.
- It supports [[wiki/concepts/Credible Sales|Credible Sales]] because persuasion starts from the other person's reality.
