# Credibility Beats Sales Tactics

## Statement

For high-quality persuasion, credibility is more important than sales tactics.

## Evidence For

- [[wiki/sources/Sell the Truth|Sell the Truth]] argues that people are hardwired to resist being sold to, and that sophisticated people can see through technique.
- The source emphasizes truth, long-term thinking, expertise, simplicity, and trust as the basis of successful persuasion.
- Naval's model advises moving on when a pitch does not resonate rather than trying to break resistance.

## Evidence Against Or Tensions

- The source explicitly does not claim to solve quota-driven sales where someone must sell a fixed product on a fixed timeline.
- Tactics such as Cialdini's influence principles may still help, but the source treats them as secondary.

## Current Assessment

Supported by this source as a founder/operator communication principle. Needs more sources before being treated as a general claim about all sales environments.
