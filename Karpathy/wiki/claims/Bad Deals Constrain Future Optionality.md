# Bad Deals Constrain Future Optionality

## Statement

Bad deals are dangerous because they can constrain future options long after the immediate need has passed.

## Evidence For

- [[wiki/sources/Sell the Truth|Sell the Truth]] frames contracts as mutual constraints that close down future paths in exchange for cooperation.
- The source highlights investors, board seats, preferred stock, veto rights, and long-term partnerships as examples that can be hard to unwind.
- Naval advises starting fundraising early enough to avoid having one's back against the wall.

## Evidence Against Or Tensions

- Some deals are easy to unwind, in which case compromise may be less costly.
- The source warns against bad compromise but does not advocate refusing all pragmatic tradeoffs.

## Current Assessment

Supported as a strategic caution for long-term, hard-to-unwind commitments.
